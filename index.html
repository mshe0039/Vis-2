The NBA Greats

what are the greats of the NBA

Coming up to the 2022 NBA all-star game, discussions on the all-time NBA greats has re-arsine, with discussions on the best NBA teams, players, and most important position being hotly debated. To give insight into these discussions, the factors of player state, position, age and a player side-by-side comparison were investigated. These questions on the greats requires the analysis of all areas of play, both offensively and defensively and as such, to give insight into the greatest of the last 2 decades, the states of average points, average goals, average 3 and 2 pointers, average free throws and average fouls were considered.

When comparing which state has produces the best NBA talent over the last 2 decades, adjusting for the number of teams in the state, Washington can be seen to perform far better across all the statistics considered, with Washington players scoring on average 755 points per round, compared to Massachusetts, the state with the next highest average points per round, of 629 points.

On the contrary, North Carolina can be seen to be lacking across all statistics, with players from this state scoring an average of 68 goals per round, compared to the next worst of Tennessee of 106 goals per round. From this, Washington can be said to have been the greatest NBA team of the last 2 decades, with respect to player quality

Impact of Age

In comparing the players by age across all the statistics considered, across all years, a higher density of younger players can be performing better than older players, as to be expected as players age. Kobe Bryant is an exception of this, with an average point per game of 27 at the age of 34 in 2012, while the in 2003, during his prime age at 25, his average points per game was below this a 24. A similar trend can be seen with another all-star player in LeBron James, with his average points per game within a range of 25 and 31 points between 2008 and 2021, where his maximum of 31 points per game was scored at the age of 37. As shown, age can dictate an all-stars performance, with younger players more densely populating the list, with on average, a higher score in all states with some exceptions with particularly great players

The Greatest Position?

From comparing the positions across both states, the middle 50% of all shooting guards outperformed all other positions across both stats. Looking at the extremes now, both point guards and center outperform shooting guards. while true, these positions have a far greater spread in performance, lending to these extremes being outliers. As can be seen in the figure, the positions closer to net generally score more than other positions, with the median points per round of small forwards and power forwards being 23.4 and 22 points respectively, with only shooting guards competing with an average point per game of 24. Thus, an argument can be made that the greatest players over the last 2 decades can be seen to be shooting guards

The Greatest Players?

Finally comparing players over the years across different stats, many players can be seen to be the greatest in some regard. James Harden currently holds the highest average points per game in 2018 of 36 possibly making him the best scorer. Moreover, James Harden holds the greatest average free throws made per game of 10 free throws in 2019. While defensively, Ben Wallace has the most blocks per game of in 2002 of 3.1 blocks per game, making him a defensive great. On the other hand, being the greatest in a stat does mean they are the greatest all round player, with players such as Kobe Bryant and LeBron James boasting very impressive average stats per game over a period of over 10 years, giving these two players an argument for being the greatest of all time
